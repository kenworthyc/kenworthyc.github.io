rough drafts for programming

Version control is a way for anyone to keep track of all the steps they've taken on a creative project on a computer. "Version control" is particularly popular with programmers, but it's an idea you may have come across as early as elementary school. If you ever had to turn in an outline for a short essay, then turn in a rough draft, and lastly a final version, you essentially broke a task down into parts, had copies of your work on each step, and then completed the task. *Version control makes it easy to track all your changes on a project*, which protects each stage, and lets you reference each change at any point.
